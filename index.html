My interest in technology began like it has for a lot of folks born in the 80's. I was eleven years old when we inherited our first computer, which through happenstance became my own very quickly. I was fixated. Shortly after, I was hired as a pre-teen to design and develop an e-commerce site for an ice skating manufacturer in my hometown.
Fast forwarding through working in a print shop, then earning my degree as a designer and photographer while running a small painting business, my drive and experimentation has opened doors for me to join and create many successful product teams as a founder, director, manager, product designer, and a developer.

Career Objective

I am humbled and grateful that millions depend on my handiwork. Eager to contribute my experience to a thoughtful, diverse, and collaborative team, I aim to enhance their products and bring out the best in our collective efforts.

Delivered as Make&Model

  • Imperfect Foods: Collaborated during a growth phase to revitalize the design and content of their marketing website, e-commerce design patterns, and developed a product roadmap for their future, contributing to a kinder, less wasteful world and leading to their acquisition by Misfits Market. Our work together led to a 17% decrease in cancellations, a 20-30% decrease in first order bounces, and a 10-12% increase in first and second average order value. ⟶ Case study.
  • CVision: I directed the design of an open-source software experience for marine biologists to transform huge volumes of oceanic footage into meaningful data for AI training, which would at times used for piloting autonomous underwater vehicles. CVision was funded by NOAA and later lead to our work with MBARI (below). Their training and reporting product is now the standard for AI-driven oceanic research. ⟶ Case study.

  • Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute: Following the creation of CVision's product, I directed the creation of FathomNet, a tool utilized by the marine sciences community for sharing and exploring oceanographic data, as well as aiding in research and education in understanding the marine environment. Think about it as a public database alike iNaturalist for the marine environment. This tool is now utilized by research institutes across the world to unite and improve their observational datasets. ⟶ Case study.
  • NAIS: In a series of engagements with NAIS, I orchestrated the delivery of their new MarketView, DASL, and Compensation Explorer products. Their focus is to allow folks in the business of running independent schools to better assess the trends of their addressable market as well as their spend on attaining new students, to compare and understand performance differences between schools, and to assure appropriately competitive compensation for employees. ⟶ Case study.
  • Better Place Forests: A past client who joined BPF as their CMO had asked me to bring in my team to “upcycle” their brand, to reconsider their entire marketing site content strategy, and to deliver an e-commerce experience designed to help make end-of-life planning more approachable, affordable, and environmentally sound. The relationship proved to be a huge success, enabling their expansion into midwest and eastern forests, and reducing decision friction and the difficulty around discussing death. ⟶ Case study.
